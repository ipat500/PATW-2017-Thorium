LFTR Advantages: Molten Salt Coolant

  1. Reactor runs at atmospheric pressure
  2. Fluorides (FLiBe or FLiNaK) are chemically stable
  3. Dangerous fission products (e.g. Iodine-131 and Cesium-137) can't escape as gases
  4. Wide safe temperature range:
    • Melting point: 459°C
    • Boiling point: 1430°C
  5. Side benefits of high output temperature (700°C)
    • Closed cycle gas turbine
    • Desalinating water

Solid Fuel Disadvantages

  1. Expensive fabrication
  2. Fuel must be shuffled around the reactor
  3. Inefficient fuel burn-up

LFTR Advantages: Liquid Fuel

  1. Chemical processing (e.g. Uranium extraction via Fluorination)
  2. Efficient fuel burn
  3. Homogeneity
  4. Removal of neutron poisons (e.g. Xenon)
  5. Useful fission products can be extracted for other uses
  6. Freeze plug and drain tank

LFTR Advantages: Thorium

  1. Abundant
  2. Waste product from Rare Earth Mining
  3. Isotopic enrichment is not necessary
  4. Non-proliferating

    5. Th-232 + neutron => Pa-233 => 27 days => U-233

    U-233 + neutron => Fission products + Energy + more neutrons

LFTR Advantages: Summary

  1. Improved safety
  2. Non-proliferating fuel cycle
  3. Small modular design - Can be produced in a factory
  4. Efficient fuel utilization
  5. Cheap abundant Thorium fuel
  6. Minimal nuclear waste production
  7. Existing nuclear waste recycling (debatable)

Oak Ridge National Laboratories Molten Salt Reactor Experiment (MSRE)

  1. Powered on during 1965 - 1969
  2. Successfully demonstrated working with Flouride based Nuclear Fuel salts
  3. Successfully demonstrated the passive safety systems
  4. U-235 operation: 9005 hours
  5. U-233 operation: 4,167 hours (part of the Thorium cycle)
  6. Further information on MSRE is available in scanned documents from the project: MSRE document archive

Possible Reasons LFTR hasn't been adopted yet

  • US Navy did early mover costs for PWR
  • MSRE was abandoned before safety was a primary concern (Before 3 Mile Island, Chernobyl etc.)
  • Industry and Political Inertia
  • Incumbents have very little incentive to innovate
  • Razor blade model

Who's working on this now?

  • TerrestrialEnergy - Factory produced reactors shipped to site
  • Flibe Energy - modular reactors designs
  • Thorcon - ship based reactors
  • Shanghai Institute of Applied Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences
  • Transatomic Power (only MSR not Thorium)
  • India have expressed interest in replacing fast breeder programs with MSR
  • Lots of new work being done on molten salt cooled reactor designs (e.g. pebble based) - transferable knowledge

Potential Issues?

  • Materials must withstand corrosive salt environment
  • Materials must withstand embrittlement due to neutrons
  • Tritium problem
  • System maintenance (salt pumps etc.)
